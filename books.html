Meditations

Not the first Stoicism book I've read but the most impactful. It profoundly altered the ways I look at life and taught me to not only appreciate what I have but also stay detached so that I can maintain a sober persepective during hard times. I'm not religious, but this book is my equivalent of the Bible.

Antifragile

Another book that profoundly change my ways of looking at life and hardships. Being antifragile means to not only withstand the struggle but to thrive from it.

Sapiens

An eye-opening history lesson of how we develop as a specie. Coorporations, capitalism, human rights are all what the author called imagined orders - beliefs that are shared between millions of our fellow homosapiens, and it's one important tool we possess that help us drive significant progress.



Algorithm to Live By

This book ignite my interest in computer science. It gave a variety of examples where algorithmic thinking could prove helpful, and it's the kind of thinking that I want to adopt to become more efficient.

The Courage to Be Disliked

This book provide great ideas as food for thoughts: your past does not determine who you are and that it's not external factors prevent you from making the change you want, but rather you yourself not wanting to make that change because you're subconsciously comfortable with how things are. This is only part of the bigger picture the book, and it's one of those book that I'd need to reread to fully comprehend the message.

Man's Search for Meaning

A man who suffered through Nazi's concentration camp recalled what kept him motivated and hopeful. It put things into perspective and taught me that the way to have a fulfilled life is to strive for something bigger than yourself.
